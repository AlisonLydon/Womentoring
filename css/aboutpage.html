About Womentoring
Rosalie works for City Year UK as the Digital Marketing Officer. She graduated from Oxford University with a 2:1 in English Literature in 2017. After University, Rosalie pursued her passion for education and social mobility by interning at an educational non-profit in New York that supports low-income New York high school students in their pursuit of higher education. She then worked as an analyst at a public policy consultancy firm focused on education and employment policy before returning to the charity sector. Rosalie was greatly drawn to the opportunity to which allows her to support volunteers to develop personally and professionally. Alison currently works for City Year UK, a youth social action and education charity. She joined City Year UK in 2016 as a volunteer mentor in Manchester, she now works in fundraising. Alison has a degree in Psychology from the University of Sheffield and is passionate about volunteering, mentoring and improving educational outcomes for young people who need it most. She is delighted that womentoring will allow young women to gain a unique access to female role models in industries where women are underrepresented.

Womentoring was founded in 2018 by Rosalie and Alison, who saw the need for more active support opportunities for women working in male dominated industries.

Womentoring helps young women who are at the beginning of their careers by providing them with female role models who are further along in their careers in the same industries.

We believe that we need to go beyond merely eliminating sexist behaviours in male dominated sectors to accelerating women's careers by providing them with the opportunity for female role models to personally and professionally mentor them.

Womentoring aims to combat the gender pay gap, grow women's professional confidence and increase the number of women in leadership positions.
